How RSA prime generation works: To generate an RSA-1024-bit key, two primes p and q of ~512 bits each must be found. By the Prime Number Theorem, roughly 1 in every 355 candidates at this size is prime. Each candidate is tested with the chosen primality algorithm until both p and q are found.
